Our Story

Saffron is mostly grown in Kashmir and distributed throughout India by a number of middlemen, therefore, it is costly and frequently out of reach for many. The risk of adulteration is also raised by this intricate supply chain. A direct-to-consumer business strategy that guarantees affordability, purity, and equitable returns for farmers is thus, absolutely needed.

Our saffron cultivation project uses a simple yet effective setup involving a temperature controller and a fermentation chamber with a cooling mat to create optimal growth conditions. This low-cost, energy-efficient method allows off-season and region-independent cultivation of high-quality saffron. Unlike traditional farming, it ensures controlled temperature for consistent yield and purity without pesticides or advanced tech. The solution tackles commercial issues like limited growing seasons, high production costs, and low accessibility, making saffron farming more profitable, scalable, and feasible even in non-traditional areas.

The system is energy-efficient, scalable, and reduces manual effort through automation. This innovation promotes sustainable agriculture, lowers operational costs, and creates new opportunities for farmers, making saffron cultivation more accessible and profitable.
